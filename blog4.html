WHY IT AND NOT EDUCATION

Choosing a college course is a big decision, and for me, it wasn't straightforward. Originally, my top choices were different from Information Technology (IT), but due to family reasons, I ended up in IT at ISU, even though it was my last option out of five.

One of the questions I often get from my instructors is why I didn't go for Education, especially considering my knack for engaging presentations. I even walk around while explaining and surprise my listeners with questions.

My enthusiasm for these activities might make it seem like Education would be a perfect fit for me. The truth is, even though I excel at engaging an audience, I didn't choose Education because I realized that I don't have the patience required for teaching, particularly to younger students. Teaching requires a lot of dedication and patience, qualities I found lacking in myself when I considered working in that field.

So, while IT wasn't my first choice, it became a practical and fitting option given my circumstances. I might not have chosen Education, but I'm making the most of my IT journey, applying my creativity and reporting skills in new ways.
